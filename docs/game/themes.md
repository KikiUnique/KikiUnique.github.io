The themes of {{site.gameName}} revolve around survival, community, and negotiation.

### Survival ###
The world has been overtaken by the plants, environments, creatures of the Otherworld turning it into a dangerous place to live in at the best of times. The Fey of the Otherworld make regular journeys to the World to trade, make deals, and take what they need. The fey are predatory and the large majority of them consider humans to be beneath them. Most events take place in a place that is treated as a safe place or neutral zone of sorts but your character still needs to survive between events during downtime. During events you will need to make sure you have everything you need in order to keep yourself safe during downtime.

### Community ###
With the sudden drop in the human population the easiest way to survive the changes, creatures, and the Fey return was to form a group and work together. After the Great Wild Hunt from the fey return many of these communities stayed together to keep each other safe. These groups have worked together to build settlements, defenses, and deals in order to keep their people safe. Being part of a group will make survival easier as you have more people working together giving you greater access to the items and materials you need to be safe.

### Negotiation ###
The fey are fond of making deals imbued with magic that holds all participants to the deal. Deals should be made with caution as Fey can disguise themselves of have others bargain on their behalf. Any agreement could be with a fey. The Fey need to actively expend power to make a deal. Making deals with the Fey is often a both positive and negative and you might be dealing with a Fey without realising it. For this reason many humans are very careful about accepting a deal from anyone without first negotiating terms.
With the humans becoming more cautious regarding deals the Fey don't try to trick humans into deals very often. Deals are made clear and done openly but the Fey will still try to sneak clever wording into their deals to gain the upper hand in the deal. You have to really upset or offend a Fey in order for them to trap you in a deal. The only time Fey will try to trick someone into a deal would be if the human has greatly offended them.
