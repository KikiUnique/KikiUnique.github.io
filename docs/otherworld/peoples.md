## The Otherworlds Inhabitants ##
The native people of the Otherworld are the Fey, creatures who are part of the magic that permeates their world. Many Fey are always trying to gather enough power to elevate their position through deals, traded power, and expanding their influence over more people and places. This vying for power has lead to the Fey have a layered society with some being above others. The High Fey rules over domains, the Low Fey live in domains under the domination of a High Fey. Below this are the Humans and Feylings that live within the domains. Finally there are the Wild Fey that live in the Wilds around the domains. They are free of the High Fey and work to keep their freedom.

### The High Fey
The High Fey are the fey so powerful and influential that their mere presence has shaped a space within the Otherworld that reflects their nature in the world around them. Fey trade and gain power through the bargains they make and keep and the power they are granted by others. Their domains are each unique although some may appear superficially similar, these similarities in domain can often show those that are conflict with others as similar domains often shows High Fey in competition for power from similar sources.
The High Fey only very rarely leave the Otherworld, many of them left for the hunt for the Great Wild Hunt, but they all returned once the hunt was concluded. Now you are more likely to encounter a glamour or underling of the High Fey in the World if something catches their attention.
 The High Fey with active underlings in the area includes The Court of Twilight, The Midnight Conclave, The Teller of Lies, The Bone Guard, The Lost, and The Dark Burning Sun 

### The Low Fey
The Low Fey are those Fey that are aligned to a High Fey. They have a place in the High Feys domain. Some owe a debt to the High Fey, others have pledged allegiance, and some have come into being when the domain came to being. The Low Fey are influenced and have their behaviours affected by their High Fey, causing pain or discomfort should they try to go against their High Feys influence.
The Low Fey vary in power with some being very powerful in their own right. Whilst they will often work on behalf of the High Fey they have their own schemes and work to get power over other Low Fey in their domain. It can be difficult to tell if a Low Feys Schemes are for their own benefit or their High Feys benefit but this is because often it is both.
The High Fey active in the area have holdings being operated and maintained by Low Fey in the local area.

### The Wild Fey
The Wild Fey are those Fey creatures that have not been drawn to a High Fey or managed to avoid owing a debt. They are free in their actions as they can't be influenced by the High Fey and aren't powerful enough to manifest a domain which restricts their behaviours in other ways. These fey make deals for their own benefit but can not be trusted as their natures tend to be flighty and easily changed. The Wild Fey may not be the most powerful, but they can still be very much dangerous.
The Wild Fey have a natural affinity for finding paths through the wilds to and from the High Fey domains and the World. They often enter the World for their own purposes.

### Feylings and Humans
In the Otherworld are many Humans and Feylings. Some are travellers passing through the Wild Paths but the greater number of those serving the High Fey and some of the more powerful of the Low Fey. These are the humans that are taken by the Fey. What they are doing in the Otherworld depends on the domain they find themselves bound to. Some are there as sport for the Fey, some are slaves, servants, pets, or event agents recruited to work with the Fey. The Fey are not human and their use of enchantment for tricks, jokes, and daily life can make the lives of those bound difficult, even for those bound to the friendliest of the High Fey.
The humans and Feylings in the domains or bound to a Fey may not leave unless they are given permission from the fey they are bound to.
